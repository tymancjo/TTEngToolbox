Reliability Assessment

Ageing Physics Rate doubles for every 10°C over RTI. Duty cycling reduces cumulative thermal damage.
Failure Logic 50% retention is the UL defined "End of Life". Mechanical failure often precedes electrical failure.

Mathematical Methodology & Formulas

1. The Arrhenius Acceleration Factor (\(A_F\))

The tool uses the "10-degree rule" which is an engineering simplification of the Arrhenius equation. It assumes the chemical reaction rate (aging) doubles for every 10°C increase in temperature over the Relative Thermal Index (RTI).

$$A_F = 2^{\frac{T_{op} - RTI}{10}}$$

2. Time-Weighted Duty Cycle (\(A_{F,eff}\))

For variable loads, we calculate the cumulative damage by averaging the acceleration factors based on the time spent at each temperature state.

$$A_{F,eff} = \left( A_{F,peak} \times \frac{Hours_{Peak}}{24} \right) + \left( A_{F,base} \times \frac{Hours_{Base}}{24} \right)$$

3. Property Retention Over Time (\(P_t\))

UL defines the RTI as the temperature where the material retains 50% of its properties after 100,000 hours (\(L_{ref} \approx 11.41\) years). The remaining health percentage is modeled as an exponential decay:

$$P(t) = 100 \times 0.5^{\left( \frac{t}{L_{effective}} \right)} \quad \text{where} \quad L_{effective} = \frac{11.41 \text{ years}}{A_{F,eff}}$$

4. End of Life (EOL) Prediction

The EOL metric displayed represents the specific year where the 50% retention threshold is crossed for the most critical (lowest) RTI property provided.

$$EOL \text{ (Years)} = \frac{11.41}{A_{F,eff}}$$
