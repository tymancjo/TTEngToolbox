ThunderStroke V6 - High Fidelity Engine

ThunderStroke V6 is a thermodynamic simulator designed to calculate pressure and temperature transients in electrical equipment during internal arc faults. It leverages a non-ideal gas model to account for thermal effects at extreme temperatures.

How it Works

The engine uses a Mass and Energy Balance approach, integrating differential equations for each compartment defined in your geometry. The key improvement in V6 is the move away from the "Constant Specific Heat" assumption.

Equation of State: P = (m * R * T) / V
Internal Energy: U = m * Cv(T) * T

Physical Enhancements

  • Variable Cv(T): As temperature rises, vibrational modes of air molecules activate, absorbing more energy. This effectively "buffers" the pressure rise.
  • Boltzmann Radiation: Energy is lost to compartment walls proportional to T4. This is critical for modeling energy saturation.
  • Refined Arc Model: Full 3-phase power simulation with dynamic current limiting (kb factor).

Using the Tool

  1. Editor Tab: Graphically layout your system. Add volumes, define their names/sizes, and draw connections (orifices) between them.
  2. Parameters: Set the Irms (Current), Uarc (Voltage), and Tarc (Burning time).
  3. Run: Click the "Run" button to execute the simulation. The data is instantly shared between the Editor and Simulator.
  4. Results: Switch to the Simulator tab to view charts and summary data.

Under the Hood

The simulation uses a high-resolution time-stepping algorithm (default 500ns) to ensure numerical stability during the rapid expansion phase of the arc fault.
