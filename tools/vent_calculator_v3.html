In industrial low-voltage switchgear (LV SWGR) design under IEC 61439-1/-2, thermal validation is highly regulated to guarantee continuous uptime and protect against busbar flashovers or breakdown of joint coatings. This tool improves on the constant-R approximation by incorporating the IEC 60890 natural convection nonlinearity.

Standard Temperature Limits (IEC 61439-1, Table 6):
  • Bare Copper Joint Interfaces: 90°C
  • Tin-Plated Joints: 105°C
  • Silver- or Nickel-Plated Joints: 115°C
  • Manual operating handles: 70°C (metallic)
Why NC correction matters:

Natural convection: $h_{NC} \propto \Delta T^{0.25}$, so $P_{NC} \propto \Delta T^{1.25}$ (not linear). The constant-R approximation assumes $P \propto \Delta T$ (exponent 1), which underestimates derating when ambient rises — giving a non-conservative (optimistic) result for ambient-sensitive applications.

1. Baseline Thermal Power Budget

At rated conditions ($I_0$, $T_{amb0}$, $T_{obj0}$), total Joule loss equals total cooling power. We define baseline cooling power $P_0 = R \cdot I_0^2$ and split it between two parallel physical mechanisms via the convective fraction $k$:

$$P_0 = \underbrace{k \cdot P_0}_{\text{NC boundary layer}} + \underbrace{(1-k) \cdot P_0}_{\text{bulk advection}}$$

2. Parallel Heat Path Scaling

Each mechanism scales differently with airflow ratio $x = \text{Flow}/\text{Flow}_0$ and temperature headroom $\Delta T = T_{obj} - T_{amb}$:

$$P_{NC}(x,\Delta T) = k \cdot P_0 \cdot x^n \cdot \left(\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta T_0}\right)^{1.25}$$ $$P_{FC}(x,\Delta T) = (1-k) \cdot P_0 \cdot x \cdot \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta T_0}$$

NC exponent 1.25 from IEC 60890 ($h_{NC} \propto \Delta T^{0.25}$, so $P_{NC} \propto \Delta T^{1.25}$). Bulk advection is linear in both flow and $\Delta T$ — exact for enthalpy transport.

3. The v3 Current Translation Formula

Thermal equilibrium requires $R \cdot I^2 = P_{NC} + P_{FC}$. Dividing by $R \cdot I_0^2 = P_0$ eliminates the unknown electrical resistance $R$:

$$I(T_{amb}, x) = I_0 \cdot \sqrt{k \cdot x^n \cdot \left(\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta T_0}\right)^{1.25} + (1-k) \cdot x \cdot \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta T_0}}$$

At $x=1$, $\Delta T = \Delta T_0$ (rated point): $I = I_0 \cdot \sqrt{k + (1-k)} = I_0$ ✓. For ambient-only change ($x=1$): effective exponent on $\Delta T/\Delta T_0$ lies between 0.5 (pure bulk, $k \to 0$) and 0.625 (pure NC, $k \to 1$) — matching IEC 61439-1 empirical formula.

Comparison with constant-R (v2) model:

v2: $I = I_0 \sqrt{(\Delta T/\Delta T_0) / (k/x^n + (1-k)/x)}$ — temperature axis exponent always 0.5. v3: exponent weighted by $k$, ranges 0.5–0.625. Difference is small for flow-axis changes but significant for ambient-only: at +10°C ambient rise (55 K→45 K headroom), v2 gives ~9.6% derating vs v3 ~11–12% (closer to IEC 61439-1 empirical 12.2%).
